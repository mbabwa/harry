## I help people find clarity in fuzzy situations by making knowledge visible and usable.

I help people work together on critical challenges with greater clarity and purpose. I use visuals and design to guide problem framing and solving toward meaningful action.

My background is in information design, but over the course of my twenty-six-year career I’ve expanded into strategic design and systems thinking. I have worked in large and small design agencies, across strategy, branding, and communication design. In 2013, I started Sense Information Design but have been exploring other models and engagements to do work that interests and challenges me.

I am co-author with [Sheila Pontis](https://www.sheilapontis.com/) of [Information Design Unbound](https://www.infodesignunbound.com), a comprehensive intro text on design for understanding.

Check my [now](/now) page for the latest updates.

## Focus

I believe that our ability to tackle our most difficult challenges today depends on how well we can work with knowledge, ideas, and information — individually and collaboratively. As we deal with more unfamiliar situations and more entangled problems, we face greater cognitive demands not only to make sense of what's happening and help others understand, but to readily think up new possibilities and test them out so we can identify the best paths forward.

My work spans four areas:

- **Making sense** of real-time conversations, content, and research material using an array of skills, from structuring and diagramming to deep analysis and synthesis.

- **Enabling understanding** through the planning and design of communication artifacts and experiences that respond to different audience’s needs and contexts.

- **Cultivating imagination** by raising awareness of attitudes and behaviors that help and hinder open thinking, and helping people build skill in generating ideas fluently.

- **Exploring and expressing ideas vividly** in a variety of visual, tactile, and experiential forms.


## Skills

- **Visualization:** I guide and draw conversations in real time to uncover, capture, and synthesize people’s thoughts. Drawings evolve as new facts and ideas emerge, in sketch and digital form. They can be incorporated into stories, information displays, and other communication design artifacts. 

- **Facilitation:** I design and lead collaborative sessions at key stages of the problem solving process. Activities have a clear purpose, get everyone involved, and produce new ideas that inform what’s next.

- **Design:** I create artifacts to help people communicate clearly, from diagrams and stories to publications and information environments. Close collaboration is an important part of the design process.
